# Showcase prompts

These ten public prompts are the complete Pages showcase set. They describe
repository-local work; none implies a hosted agent, live backend, or released
artifact.

1. **One idea to public product.** — Trace one idea through reviewed source, CUBBY, rapplication, egg, controller, isolated twin, signed twin-chat, owner iMessage, and exact-commit release preparation.
2. **Map the whole RAPP system.** — Explain every selected local stage and clearly separate implemented components from mapped neighborhood, fleet, and cloud references.
3. **Verify this repository.** — Run the context, catalog, Pages, privacy, unit, and repository gates and summarize only evidence produced by those checks.
4. **Build the deterministic artifact chain.** — Fetch only locked inert dependencies, build twice with one revision and epoch, and prove every output byte is equal.
5. **Hatch an isolated twin.** — Verify a CUBBY egg, hatch it offline into separate workspace and state roots, inspect its receipt, and stop before starting services.
6. **Explain signed twin-chat.** — Walk through identity binding, P-256 signatures, freshness, durable replay handling, nonce binding, and signed responses without exposing private state.
7. **Prepare owner-only iMessage.** — Follow the fresh-fork tutorial through prerequisites and content-free preflight, leaving enrollment and live sending for the release operator.
8. **Inspect capability ownership.** — Use the implementation and capability matrices to show who owns all selected capabilities and why reference-only entries are not local claims.
9. **Trace evidence to implementation.** — Start with one direct-evidence capability, follow its context route, and identify its tested code, schema, decision, runbook, and remaining gap.
10. **Prepare an exact-commit release.** — Validate version and tag, build and hatch twice from one clean commit, prepare external sidecars, and stop before publication unless every gate is proven.
